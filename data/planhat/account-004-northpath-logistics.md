## 2026-04-17 — Expansion scoping with CRO Anneke de Vries

Met with Anneke to scope the Belgian business unit onboarding (12 new seats, targeting go-live in June). Conversation started on the expansion logistics but shifted to platform readiness concerns. Anneke said her NL team has essentially learned to ignore the View activity panel on quotes — their playbook is "send the quote, wait 24 hours, pick up the phone regardless of what the system says." She described it as a workaround they've internalized so deeply it doesn't even register as friction anymore.

The concern is about the Belgian team. They're coming in fresh with no institutional memory of the platform's quirks. Anneke said: "My NL reps know to ignore 'Offline' and call anyway. But if I onboard 12 new people in Brussels and they see 'Offline' on a quote, they're going to assume the customer hasn't engaged. They'll wait. We'll lose deal velocity in the first quarter and then I'll have an adoption problem."

She also raised a second issue I hadn't heard before: multi-quote deals. NorthPath frequently sends three quote variants to the same buyer — standard, premium, and custom configurations. On the deal page, all three appear in the Quotations table, each with their own View activity popover. But there's no way to see at a glance which variant the buyer is actually spending time on without clicking into each one individually. Anneke said: "I want to know that the buyer opened the premium quote four times and the standard quote once. That tells me where to steer the conversation. Right now I have to click View activity on each row, one by one, and mentally compare."

She tied it back to the Belgian expansion: new reps won't know to do that manual comparison. They'll either ignore the data entirely (like the NL team) or make bad assumptions based on incomplete views. Either way, it undermines the value prop she used internally to justify the expansion seats.

## 2026-04-03 — Pre-expansion readiness check (internal prep)

Ran through NorthPath's adoption metrics ahead of the expansion conversation. NL team usage is strong — 89% weekly active on deal management, 94% quote creation through the platform. But quote activity panel engagement is notably low: only 23% of reps click View activity more than once per week. Cross-referenced with Anneke's previous comment that her team "doesn't bother checking it." The data confirms the workaround is widespread.

Noted for the expansion plan: if we're adding 12 Belgian seats and the existing team's best practice is "ignore the activity panel," we risk training the new cohort on a degraded workflow from day one. Flagging this as a potential adoption risk for the expansion QBR.
