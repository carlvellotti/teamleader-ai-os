### Confidence Indicators

- The CSM's tone is factual and neutral: "He wasn't angry — more resigned." This is CSM editorial interpretation of Henrik's emotional state.

## Account: Axis SaaS
**Tier:** Enterprise | **ARR:** EUR 165,000 | **Seats:** 48 | **Health:** Yellow | **CSM:** James Walker

### Key Claims
- **Claim**: A senior AE (Mark Janssen) maintains a personal spreadsheet to track quote opens because he considers the platform's activity column unreliable. He reconstructs engagement data from email read receipts, phone call notes, and direct prospect messages.
  - **Quote** (CSM paraphrase of what Marcia said Mark told her): "the activity column is wrong half the time."
  - **Source**: account-002-axis-saas.md, 2026-04-07 call with VP Sales Ops Marcia Engstrom. Note: this is second-hand — CSM reports Marcia reporting Mark's words. The "half the time" phrasing may be Mark's, Marcia's, or a paraphrase by either.

- **Claim**: The View activity popover sometimes shows "Offline" for quotes that were opened (prospects reference specific line items in follow-ups), and sometimes shows "Viewed" with timing that doesn't match when the prospect says they looked at it. The Status column (Open/Sent/Accepted) works correctly for macro lifecycle; the engagement layer inside the activity popover is what's unreliable.
  - **Quote**: No verbatim quote; CSM paraphrase of Marcia's elaboration.
  - **Source**: account-002-axis-saas.md, 2026-04-07.

- **Claim**: Junior reps take the activity data at face value and make follow-up decisions based on it. If the data says "Offline," they wait. The VP Sales Ops has started telling new hires to ignore the panel and call anyway.
  - **Quote** (CSM paraphrase of Marcia): "call anyway, don't trust the panel"
  - **Source**: account-002-axis-saas.md, 2026-04-07. CSM attributes this as what Marcia tells new hires. It is not clear whether this is Marcia's exact wording or the CSM's compression.

- **Claim**: In the quarterly friction survey, Marcia named the activity panel on quotes as her team's single biggest frustration and ranked "Quote engagement visibility" as #1 feature improvement request.
  - **Quote** (direct, from written survey response): "The activity panel on quotes. My team needs to know when a prospect opens a quote so they can time their follow-up. The data exists somewhere in CloudSign but it doesn't reliably surface on the deal page. We see 'Offline' for quotes we know were opened. Our best AE keeps a separate spreadsheet because he doesn't trust it. That spreadsheet shouldn't need to exist."
  - **Source**: account-002-axis-saas.md, 2026-03-19 quarterly friction survey. This is a direct written response from Marcia, the highest-provenance quote in the Planhat data set.

### Stated Preferences vs. Described Behaviors
- **Gap between stated purpose and actual usage**: Marcia wants the team to use activity data to time follow-ups (stated preference). In practice, she is actively coaching her team to ignore the data and call regardless ("call anyway, don't trust the panel"). This is a direct contradiction: she wants the tool to drive behavior but is training her team to bypass it.
- **Top performer vs. rest of team**: Mark (top performer) has built a parallel system (spreadsheet) and works around the tool entirely. Junior reps, by contrast, either trust the panel and make poor follow-up decisions or are now being told to ignore it. Neither group is using the tool as designed.

### Confidence Indicators
- Marcia's written survey response is definitive and specific: "doesn't reliably surface," "we see 'Offline' for quotes we know were opened," "That spreadsheet shouldn't need to exist." No hedging language.
- The "wrong half the time" claim (attributed to Mark via Marcia) is vague in precision. "Half the time" could be literal or hyperbolic. Its provenance is also low (second-hand through Marcia).
- The CSM editorially notes the Yellow health status is "partly because of unrelated onboarding delays," making clear the trust issue is a contributing factor but not the sole driver.

  - **Source**: account-003-meridian-financial.md, 2026-04-10.

They want the deal page to be the single source of truth; it isn't, so they compensate with manual work.

### Confidence Indicators
- Jean-Paul's language is definitive: "an unacceptable gap." No hedging.
- The claims are backed by documentary evidence: internal audit findings, CloudSign admin logs, client IT team confirmations. This is the most evidence-rich account in the data set.
- The 15-20 minute estimate and 40-deal-per-quarter scope are Jean-Paul's numbers relayed by the CSM. The CSM does not editorialize or qualify them.
- All "quotes" in this account are short phrases embedded in CSM narrative. The full meeting was described by the CSM, not transcribed. Provenance is CSM paraphrase of Jean-Paul's statements.

## Account: NorthPath Logistics
**Tier:** Enterprise | **ARR:** EUR 195,000 | **Seats:** 54 | **Health:** Green (expansion) | **CSM:** Thijs van Leeuwen

### Key Claims
- **Claim**: The NL team has fully internalized a workaround: "send the quote, wait 24 hours, pick up the phone regardless of what the system says." They no longer register this as friction — it's just how they work.
  - **Quote**: No verbatim quote for the workaround description; CSM paraphrase of Anneke de Vries (CRO).
  - **Source**: account-004-northpath-logistics.md, 2026-04-17 expansion scoping call.

- **Claim**: The Belgian expansion (12 new seats, June go-live) creates an adoption risk because new reps will not have the institutional memory to know the activity panel is unreliable. They will either trust "Offline" and wait (losing deal velocity) or learn to ignore the panel entirely.
  - **Quote**: "My NL reps know to ignore 'Offline' and call anyway. But if I onboard 12 new people in Brussels and they see 'Offline' on a quote, they're going to assume the customer hasn't engaged. They'll wait. We'll lose deal velocity in the first quarter and then I'll have an adoption problem."
  - **Source**: account-004-northpath-logistics.md, 2026-04-17. CSM attributes this directly to Anneke.

- **Claim**: Multi-quote deals are a distinct pain point. NorthPath frequently sends three quote variants (standard, premium, custom) to the same buyer. There is no way to see at a glance which variant the buyer is engaging with — you have to click View activity on each row individually and mentally compare.
  - **Quote**: "I want to know that the buyer opened the premium quote four times and the standard quote once. That tells me where to steer the conversation. Right now I have to click View activity on each row, one by one, and mentally compare."
  - **Source**: account-004-northpath-logistics.md, 2026-04-17. CSM attributes this directly to Anneke.

- **Claim**: Adoption metrics confirm the workaround is widespread: 89% weekly active on deal management, 94% quote creation, but only 23% of reps click View activity more than once per week.
  - **Quote**: No verbatim quote; CSM internal metrics review.
  - **Source**: account-004-northpath-logistics.md, 2026-04-03 pre-expansion readiness check.

- **Claim**: The CSM flags a risk: if the existing team's best practice is "ignore the activity panel," the Belgian expansion will train new reps on a degraded workflow from day one.
  - **Quote**: No verbatim quote; CSM editorial assessment.
  - **Source**: account-004-northpath-logistics.md, 2026-04-03.

### Stated Preferences vs. Described Behaviors
- **Deep gap between stated value and actual behavior.** Anneke used the quotations engagement feature as part of her internal justification for the expansion seats (stated value prop). But her existing NL team's actual behavior is to completely ignore the activity panel (23% engagement rate, confirmed workaround). She is selling the tool's potential to her leadership while her team has already abandoned the feature in practice.
- **Multi-quote comparison is a new, distinct signal.** Anneke describes wanting to compare engagement across quote variants on a deal — this is not about data reliability (the problem in other accounts) but about information architecture. Even if all the engagement data were perfectly accurate, the current UI would still require clicking into each row's popover individually to compare. This is a separate problem from the "Offline" data reliability issue.

### Confidence Indicators
- Anneke's language is definitive and specific: she names exact scenarios ("premium quote four times, standard quote once"), exact expansion numbers (12 seats, Brussels, June), and exact consequences ("lose deal velocity," "adoption problem"). No hedging.
- The CSM's internal metrics (23% View activity engagement) provide quantitative confirmation of the workaround behavior Anneke described qualitatively. This is a strong convergence of qual + quant.
- Anneke's quotes are attributed directly by the CSM and read as verbatim recall from the meeting. They are still CSM-recorded rather than transcribed, so they are one step removed from a primary transcript.
- The CSM's editorial risk assessment (training new cohort on degraded workflow) is clearly flagged as internal analysis, not customer language.
