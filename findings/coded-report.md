Date: 2026-04-30

## Code 1: "Offline means nothing"
**Definition:** The "Offline" status label produces false negatives so frequently that experienced users treat it as meaningless — quotes confirmed viewed by customers still show "Offline" on the deal page.

**Frequency:** 3/3 sources
- Modjo: 6 of 8 calls (001, 002, 003, 004, 007, 008)
- Zendesk: 2 of 8 tickets (TKT-004, TKT-007)
- Planhat: 4 of 4 accounts

**Sentiment:** Negative — ranges from resigned ("dat offline-gedoe is een beetje verwarrend") to emphatic ("makes no sense at all")

**Strongest quotes:**
1. "If reps can't trust what they see on the deal page, they stop looking at it. Then they stop using the system for pipeline decisions." — Henrik Larsson, CRO (planhat.md, Global Industries)
2. "Bij mij staan alle drie de offertes als 'Offline.' Ik kan niet zien welke je hebt geopend en welke niet." — Anna, rep (modjo.md, call-007)
3. "The activity label next to 'View activity' shows 'Offline' which makes no sense at all since the customer obviously opened and signed the document online." — Antoine, rep (zendesk.md, TKT-007)

## Code 2: "I just call everyone after two days"
**Definition:** Reps have replaced signal-based follow-up with calendar-based follow-up — calling every customer on a fixed schedule because the engagement data can't be trusted for prioritization.

**Frequency:** 3/3 sources
- Modjo: 5 of 8 calls (001, 003, 004, 007, 008)
- Zendesk: 1 of 8 tickets (TKT-004)
- Planhat: 2 of 4 accounts (Axis SaaS, NorthPath)

**Sentiment:** Negative — resigned efficiency loss. Reps don't frame this as a complaint; they present it as pragmatic adaptation. The negativity is structural: they've given up on the tool doing its job.

**Strongest quotes:**
1. "I always call about 48 hours after sending a quote. It's become a habit because, honestly, the tracking in our system isn't always reliable." — Pieter, rep (modjo.md, call-003)
2. "My NL reps know to ignore 'Offline' and call anyway. But if I onboard 12 new people in Brussels and they see 'Offline' on a quote, they're going to assume the customer hasn't engaged. They'll wait. We'll lose deal velocity in the first quarter." — Anneke de Vries, CRO (planhat.md, NorthPath)
3. "If I know someone's read it three times, I know they're interested and I can be more direct. But right now it's a black box." — Maaike, rep (modjo.md, call-004)

## Code 3: "That spreadsheet shouldn't need to exist"
**Definition:** Reps and ops teams have built parallel tracking systems outside the product — personal spreadsheets, CloudSign portal logins, manual cross-referencing — to compensate for data that doesn't surface on the deal page.

**Frequency:** 2/3 sources
- Zendesk: 1 of 8 tickets (TKT-007 — rep logs into CloudSign portal to verify)
- Planhat: 2 of 4 accounts (Axis SaaS — AE's spreadsheet; Meridian — manual compliance reconciliation)

**Sentiment:** Negative — frustration at wasted effort. Marcia's tone is the sharpest: she frames the spreadsheet as an indictment, not a workaround.

**Strongest quotes:**
1. "The activity panel on quotes. My team needs to know when a prospect opens a quote so they can time their follow-up. The data exists somewhere in CloudSign but it doesn't reliably surface on the deal page. We see 'Offline' for quotes we know were opened. Our best AE keeps a separate spreadsheet because he doesn't trust it. That spreadsheet shouldn't need to exist." — Marcia Engstrom, VP Sales Ops, written survey (planhat.md, Axis SaaS)
2. "I confirmed this by logging into the CloudSign portal directly... I can manually change the status but that defeats the purpose of having CloudSign integration." — Antoine, rep (zendesk.md, TKT-007)
3. Jean-Paul's compliance team cross-references deal page activity with CloudSign admin logs for every in-scope quote — 15-20 min per deal, ~40 deals/quarter = 10-13 hours of manual reconciliation per quarter (planhat.md, Meridian)

## Code 4: "Which quote did they actually read?"
**Definition:** In multi-quote deals, reps send 2-3 options but the system can't differentiate engagement across variants — there's no way to see which option the buyer prefers without asking them directly.

**Frequency:** 2/3 sources
- Modjo: 1 of 8 calls (call-007 — sharpest instance in the entire data set)
- Planhat: 1 of 4 accounts (NorthPath — systemic pattern across their deal workflow)

**Sentiment:** Negative — a mix of frustration and missed opportunity. Dennis's reaction ("dat is wel een beetje zonde") is understated Dutch; the implication is stronger than the words.

**Strongest quotes:**
1. "Dus als ik niet had verteld dat ik optie B het beste vind, had je dat niet geweten?" — Dennis, customer (modjo.md, call-007)
2. "Nee, precies. Ik had het moeten raden of gewoon alledrie opnieuw moeten doorlopen aan de telefoon." — Anna, rep (modjo.md, call-007)
3. "I want to know that the buyer opened the premium quote four times and the standard quote once. That tells me where to steer the conversation. Right now I have to click View activity on each row, one by one, and mentally compare." — Anneke de Vries, CRO (planhat.md, NorthPath)

## Code 5: "From my side it looks like we're being ignored"
**Definition:** The tracking failure creates a two-sided trust breakdown — the customer thinks the rep is ignoring their engagement, while the rep thinks the customer hasn't looked. Warm deals go cold in the gap.

**Frequency:** 2/3 sources
- Modjo: 1 of 8 calls (call-002 — a renewal that went cold)
- Planhat: 1 of 4 accounts (Global Industries — six-figure deal saved only because buyer reached out)

**Sentiment:** Negative — this is the code with the clearest revenue impact. Rachel's "just sort of left it" describes a warm deal cooling because neither side acted.

**Strongest quotes:**
1. "It's a bit frustrating because from my side it looks like we're being ignored, but apparently from your side it looks like I haven't engaged at all." — Rachel, customer (modjo.md, call-002)
2. "I did, but nobody followed up with me. I assumed Pieter could see that I'd opened it and would reach out, but I never heard anything. So I just sort of left it." — Rachel, customer (modjo.md, call-002)
3. In a six-figure APAC renewal, the buyer (Priya) confirmed reading the quote via email ("I've read through the whole thing, looks good") but the deal page showed "Offline." The deal closed only because the buyer proactively reached out — the rep had no reason to follow up based on what the system showed. (planhat.md, Global Industries)

## Code 6: "They told me to just ignore it"
**Definition:** Institutional knowledge about the panel's unreliability transfers informally from experienced reps to new hires — creating a two-tier system where veterans bypass the tool and newcomers either trust bad data or inherit the workaround on day one.

**Frequency:** 3/3 sources
- Modjo: Implicit in 5 of 8 calls — every rep has independently learned to bypass the system. Tom's closing joke crystallizes it: "weet ik nu in ieder geval dat dat niets zegt" (now I know it means nothing).
- Zendesk: 1 of 8 tickets (TKT-004 — new rep gets contradictory definitions and identical advice: ignore it)
- Planhat: 2 of 4 accounts (Axis SaaS — VP Sales Ops coaching new hires; NorthPath — Belgian expansion risk)

**Sentiment:** Negative — learned helplessness at the individual level, adoption risk at the organizational level. The sharpest framing is Anneke's: she's about to onboard 12 new reps into a workflow her existing team has already abandoned.

**Strongest quotes:**
1. "I asked two of my colleagues and they both said something different. One told me it means the quote was created but not sent via CloudSign, and the other said it just means the tracking isn't available. They both basically told me to just ignore the status and call the customer directly." — Daan, new rep (zendesk.md, TKT-004)
2. "But if I onboard 12 new people in Brussels and they see 'Offline' on a quote, they're going to assume the customer hasn't engaged. They'll wait. We'll lose deal velocity in the first quarter and then I'll have an adoption problem." — Anneke de Vries, CRO (planhat.md, NorthPath)
3. "En Claire — als je hem opent en er staat weer 'Offline' bij mij, weet ik nu in ieder geval dat dat niets zegt." — Tom, rep (modjo.md, call-008)

## Code 7: "Just show me a view count"
**Definition:** When reps articulate what they actually want, they converge on the same signal: a simple view count per quote, visible without clicking. Not dwell time, not scroll depth — just "has this been opened, and how many times."

**Frequency:** 3/3 sources
- Modjo: 1 of 8 calls (call-004 — explicit feature request with behavioral rationale)
- Zendesk: 1 of 8 tickets (TKT-008 — detailed spec from Head of Sales Ops)
- Planhat: 1 of 4 accounts (NorthPath — framed as multi-quote comparison need)

**Sentiment:** Mixed — the only code with a forward-looking, constructive dimension. Quotes express frustration at the current state but articulate a clear, specific vision of what would help. No disagreement on the shape of the solution.

**Strongest quotes:**
1. "Honestly, I wish I could just see a simple view count — like, 'this quote has been opened four times.' That would tell me so much about how engaged someone is. Instead I'm kind of guessing." — Maaike, rep (modjo.md, call-004)
2. "When I'm managing 30+ active deals and trying to prioritize follow-ups, I need to see at a glance which quotes are hot (viewed 5 times in the last 2 days) versus cold (sent a week ago, never opened). Currently that requires clicking into every single quote's activity popover one by one." — James Hartley, Head of Sales Ops (zendesk.md, TKT-008)
3. "I want to know that the buyer opened the premium quote four times and the standard quote once. That tells me where to steer the conversation." — Anneke de Vries, CRO (planhat.md, NorthPath)

## Code 8: "All buried in a popover"
**Definition:** Even when engagement data is accurate, it's hidden behind a per-quote "View activity" click — invisible at the scan level where reps actually triage their pipeline. The information architecture buries the signal regardless of data quality.

**Frequency:** 3/3 sources
- Modjo: 2 of 8 calls (call-004 — View activity shows nothing useful; call-006 — activity view shows only generic stages)
- Zendesk: 2 of 8 tickets (TKT-003 — rep used popover reactively after customer called; TKT-008 — explicit request for inline metrics)
- Planhat: 1 of 4 accounts (NorthPath — 23% View activity click rate despite 89% deal management usage)

**Sentiment:** Negative — but distinctly architectural rather than emotional. This code describes a design problem, not a data problem. The frustration is quieter than Code 1 because reps have already stopped clicking — the 23% engagement rate at NorthPath is the quantitative confirmation.

**Strongest quotes:**
1. "the actually useful engagement data — how many times the quote was viewed, when it was last opened, whether it was downloaded, and whether any feedback was left — is all buried inside a popover that only appears when you click 'View activity.'" — James Hartley, Head of Sales Ops (zendesk.md, TKT-008)
2. Only 23% of NorthPath reps click View activity more than once per week, despite 89% weekly active on deal management and 94% on quote creation. The feature exists; the team has abandoned it. (planhat.md, NorthPath)
3. "There's a 'View activity' link I can click for more detail, but even that doesn't show anything useful when the tracking doesn't pick up the view." — Maaike, rep (modjo.md, call-004)
